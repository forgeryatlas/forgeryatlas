No. 148. From 1859
Bologna – Collegiate Tribunal of First Instance

Statement of facts to be examined by the Ottoman Grand Criminal Court in Constantinople

Mr. Giuseppe Angelo Tkiades, commissioned by the Sublime Porte, who came to this State in order to carry out the aforementioned investigation concerning counterfeiting, robbery, and fraud: as in the case of Banknotes.

Since, already in April 1859, he was about to depart from this city, by means of a letter rogatory (No. 148), he was instructed so that the Sublime Porte might transmit the following documents and materials, which were deemed necessary to complete and adjudicate the ongoing proceedings, namely:

  1. At least one of the counterfeit banknotes of twenty piastres manufactured here in the house of the accused Raffaele Crudeli, of which the other accused, Facchini, confessed to having sent 600 to Constantinople to Lodovico Randaboschi, concealed within a box of toothpicks.
  2. Another of the counterfeit banknotes of the aforesaid type which were not seized in the house of Facchini, and which he himself transported and circulated in Constantinople, where he traveled together with Raffaele Randaboschi and Alessandro Donnini.
  3. The notebook containing the banknotes, which was seized by the Ottoman police from the said Facchini when he was arrested in Constantinople.
  4. The records of the proceeds seized in Turkey in connection with this same matter.
  5. The Ottoman penal laws under which crimes of this kind are punished.

To this request, the Sublime Porte did not respond with the necessary promptness. In the reply of 20 July 1859, that Ministry of Foreign Affairs, prompted by His Excellency the Minister of Grace, Justice, and Worship in Modena, through the esteemed dispatch No. 3888 of January 5 last, did not concern itself at all with either the banknotes or the notebook requested and listed above under Nos. 1, 2, and 3.

Thus, it remains entirely unknown whether they exist and whether they can be obtained; and, if not, this must be explicitly stated, so that the impossibility of completing the case file with these documents may be formally established.

With regard to the depositions of the accused detained in the prisons of Constantinople, only vague extrajudicial information was provided, whereas the proper conduct of this proceeding required that the interrogations, that is, the depositions they gave, be transmitted in certified copy, as indicated in point 5.

Finally, it is essential that the exact date be specified on which the old code ceased and the new one came into force. While extracts of the penal provisions punishing counterfeiters should be supplied in accordance with the sixth point, the period during which these codes were in force is here only indicated approximately.

Therefore, the most prompt completion is requested.

Chancery
Bologna, 13 February 1860
N. Lazzari, Head Clerk

Invitato e in ufficio comparso avanti a me il prevenuto di cui più volte in processo a f. 38 e segg. 191 e segg. il quale ha ripetuto essere Tommaso Facchini, che ammonito al vero conforme fu più volte interrogato =Sa sapere o figurarsi l’oggetto per il quale viene oggi costituito.

Rispose = Io mi trovo qui presentato perché invitato, ma veramente non so sopra di che dovrò
essere esaminato.

Interr. = Se si rammenti di quello che fece negli antecedenti suoi costituti, posso essere in grado di specificare qual parte prendessero quel certo Cesare, quelli Antonaki, Roberto Diamanti e Vincenzo Arcebugiani nella Smaltizzazione delle banconote turche, delle quali si ebbe ragione anche di lui carico.

Rispose = Nulla più di speciale io potrei dire rapporto a questa domanda, se non che ho aver
visto quelle date persone in quel certo caffè, comunicare con Raffaele Randaboschi, e così
superficiale era la mia conoscenza che io non mi affiatai neppure con loro.

Interr. = Se sia in grado di descrivere le suddette persone, e se rivedendole fosse in grado di riconoscerle, qualora anche gli fossero esibiti i loro ritratti.

Rispose = Siccome io aveva una semplicissima superficiale conoscenza dei nomi nati soggetti, non ne ho una giusta idea, e non potrei così facilmente dare i connotati, piuttosto. Dell’uno che Dell’altro; e per conseguenza mi riservo d’esternare unicamente il mio avviso allorché mi fosse
dato vederli personalmente, o mediante ritratto.

Allora si gli asservirono tre ritratti fotografici descritti nel presente incarto e contraddistinti per maggior chiarezza coi n.ri 1, 2, 3 ed osservati che Egli costituì li ebbe, richiesto poscia che ne dica:

Rispose = La fisionomia di questi ritratti non mi sembra nuova o mi pare di poterli raffigurare
per quelle persone, fatte quali superiormente ho fatto menzione, avvertendo però che non sono in caso di distinguere l’uno dall’altro e coi rispettivi nomi gli individui stessi, in vista della sempre detta superficialissima conoscenza che di loro aveva. Questo è quanto mi interessava dichiarare in relazione alla fatta mi domanda e null’altro posso aggiungere.

Dietro lettura e conferma L. C. si sottoscrisse per essere stata l’interrogata conclusa sempre conformi agli atti dalla sua Principiale

Tommaso Facchini N. Zaccari Giud. Proc.

No. 148. Since 1859. Bologna.

Copy of acts undertaken by the Collegiate Court of First Instance in Bologna, following a requisition from His Excellency the Minister of Foreign Affairs to His Serene Highness the Sultan, dated 20 July 1859, here transmitted by His Excellency the Minister of Grace, Justice and Religious Affairs in Modena, with dispatch No. 3885 of 5 January 1860.

Reggio, 9 January 1860.

In fulfillment of the requisition mentioned on the following pages, His Excellency the Minister of Grace, Justice and Religious Affairs in Modena forwarded to this Presidency of the Tribunal, whence to the Chamber handling the proceedings of Zaccari, with a specific dispatch of the 5th, No. 3885, the following documents. These are to be joined, and are hereby joined, for the regularity of the acts to the present case indicated above, namely:

  1. A dispatch from the Ministry of Foreign Affairs in Turin, dated 14 December last, No. 6066.
  2. A copy of a dispatch dated 20 July 1859 from His Excellency the Minister of Foreign Affairs to His Highness the Sultan, through the chargé d’affaires for Ottoman affairs in Naples.
  3. An extract of articles from the Ottoman Penal Code relating to the falsification of coins and forgery in writing.
  4. Another extract concerning the same type of crime from the former Ottoman Penal Code.
  5. Three photographs depicting three individuals identified as Cesare Donnini, Roberto Diamanti, and Antonaki Dragonicos, arrested in Constantinople as suspects of complicity in the criminal falsification and circulation of Turkish banknotes. These are the same crimes for which the persons arrested here are charged, and against whom proceedings are pending. It is therefore desired that they be subjected to personal identification by the said accused persons, who might recognize them. The photographic portraits are described as follows:

An extract on stamped paper, oval in shape, representing an individual dressed bareheaded, with long hair, with beard and moustache; a rather long chin, not very full, and a regular nose; dressed in a paletot (overcoat) with long trousers, with a white shirt, without a necktie; and having on the ring finger of the left hand a circular mark that appears to be a ring; of apparently youthful age, and by manner of features and regularity of physiognomy, for the purpose of identification he is marked above the head with No. 1.

Seated as the previous one, and dressed with a cap or rather a fairly large skullcap, wearing a white shirt without a necktie, a black overcoat, and light-colored trousers not of full length; and marked above the head with the number 2.

Another individual likewise portrayed in a photograph of the same size as the previous one, also seated, of apparently mature age, with a rather elongated face, regular nose, with thick moustaches and beard, dressed with a large skullcap or cap reaching to the ears, with an overcoat that appears trimmed with fur, closed at the neck, with long trousers, almost standing upright; for the same reasons as above he is distinguished above the head with No. 3.

Bologna, 8 February 1860.

Summoned and having appeared before me in office, the accused mentioned several times in the proceedings at folios 38 and following, 191 and following, who has again stated himself to be Tommaso Facchini, who, having been admonished to tell the truth, as he had been questioned several times,

Question: Does he know or can he conjecture the purpose for which he is brought here today?

Answer: I find myself here because I was summoned, but in truth I do not know on what matter I am to be examined.

Question: If he recalls what he stated in his previous appearances, whether he is able to specify what part was taken by a certain Cesare, one Antonaki, Roberto Diamanti, and Vincenzo Arcebugiani in the circulation (passing) of Turkish banknotes, for which he himself was also implicated.

Answer: I cannot say anything more specific in response to this question, except that I saw the said persons in a certain café communicating with Raffaele Randaboschi; and so superficial was my acquaintance with them that I did not even become familiar with them.

Question: Whether he is able to describe the aforesaid persons, and if, upon seeing them again, he would be able to recognize them, even if their portraits were shown to him.

Answer: Since my acquaintance with the said individuals was extremely superficial, I do not have a clear idea of them, nor could I easily give distinguishing features, either of one or the other; and consequently I reserve myself to express my opinion only when I might see them in person or by means of a portrait.

Then three photographic portraits described in the present file and marked for greater clarity with Nos. 1, 2, 3 were shown to him; and after observing them, he was asked what he had to say:

Answer: The physiognomy of these portraits does not seem unfamiliar to me, or I believe I could associate them with those persons previously mentioned; however, I note that I am not in a position to distinguish one from another or to match them with their respective names, in view of the repeatedly stated very superficial acquaintance I had with them. This is all I have to declare in relation to the question put to me, and I can add nothing further.

After reading and confirmation, the record was signed, the examination having been concluded in conformity with the acts by his Principal.

Tommaso Facchini
N. Zaccari, Examining Judge
C. Farnaini, Chief

Summoned to the office, the accused—previously mentioned at folio 10 and following—appeared before His Honor and me, and again stated that he was Giovanni Zanetti, who, having been admonished to tell the truth, was then questioned as follows:

Question: Whether he knows the reason for the present proceeding being instituted.

Answer: I suppose that this appearance of mine may also concern those Ottoman banknotes which have been the subject of many other examinations.

Question: Whether he has any personal acquaintance with a certain Cesare Vinci, Roberto Diamanti, and Antonaki Droganicos, of whom he made mention in another of his statements.

Answer: These persons whom Your Honor recalls to me, I mentioned in the course of my previous statements only because I had heard them named, especially by Raffaele Randaboschi; but here in Bologna I had never encountered them, and consequently I could not have, nor do I have, any personal acquaintance with them.

Question: Whether he knows more specifically what involvement these individuals might have had in the circulation (distribution) of the banknotes falsified here and then sent to Turkey.

Answer: On this matter as well, I cannot provide any further clarification beyond what I have already communicated to the authorities in the course of my previous statements.

Having had this read to him, he confirmed it and signed; and he was dismissed with a warning to strictly adhere to the evidentiary conditions that had been communicated to him when he was released.

Sebastiano Zanetti
N. Zaccari, Examining Judge
C. Farnaini, Chief

Bologna, 10 February 1860.

In the office, before His Honor and myself, there appeared, upon summons, another accused person, mentioned in the proceedings at folio 7 and following, who again stated himself to be Raffaele Randaboschi. Having been admonished to tell the truth, he was then questioned as follows:

Question: Whether he can conjecture the reason for his present appearance.

Answer: As soon as I received the summons, I imagined that this examination of mine must concern those Turkish banknotes about which I have been examined many other times.

Question: Whether he is able to state more specifically what part was played by Cesare Donnini, Roberto Diamanti, and Antonaki Dragonicos in the circulation (distribution) of the banknotes mentioned.

Answer: Everything I knew I have already repeated in my previous depositions, and I can add nothing further.

Question: Whether he is able to describe and recall Cesare Donnini, Roberto Diamanti, and Antonaki Dragonicos, and whether, upon seeing them in person or in portraits, he would be able to recognize them.

Answer: These three individuals could have been about 40 years of age, more or less, of medium height; some wore a beard, others thick moustaches; they dressed in the European, that is to say French, style. I cannot describe them better; but if I were to see them, even in portraits, I believe I could recognize them.

Question: Then, the three photographic portraits described in the present file and marked Nos. 1, 2, and 3 having been shown to him and carefully examined by him, he was asked what he had to say:

Answer: I have carefully observed these three photographic portraits, and I will say that the first, marked No. 1, with beard and moustache, appears to me to be that of Antonaki Dragonicos; that the second, marked No. 2, likewise with beard and moustache and with a skullcap on the head, seems to me to be that of Roberto Diamanti; and finally, that the third, marked No. 3, having thick moustaches and a large cap covering the ears, appears to me to be that of Cesare Venzi. This is all I can say in good faith; and in all other respects I refer to my previous depositions, to which I can add nothing.

After reading and confirmation, he signed, and was dismissed, bound always to the conditions imposed upon him when he was released from prison.

Raffaele Randaboschi
N. Lazzari, Examining Judge
C. Farnaini, Clerk
