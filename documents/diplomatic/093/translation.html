His Excellency Fuad Pasha to Rüstem Bey in Turin
21 March 1860
No. 3915/30

I have just received the reply of the Imperial Ministry of Police to the communication I had made to it of your report concerning the affair of the counterfeiters in Bologna, regarding which your latest report of 8 March, No. 708, has also duly reached me.

According to the tenor of this reply, specimens of both old and new caimes were, at the time and at the request of Mr. Tkiades, transmitted to Bologna, where they are still today in the hands of the local authorities, as is evident from the interrogations undergone in Bologna by the accused, copies of which have been communicated to the Ministry of Police.

It is clearly established that, moreover, the counterfeit caimes of 20 old piastres, which the judicial authorities are now requesting as material evidence, were manufactured in that city and put into circulation in Constantinople by a certain Thomaso Facchini, from whom a thief had stolen thirty-two of these same caimes contained in a wallet, as was established by that thief’s confession. It is true that Thomaso Facchini, who was arrested following the thief’s confession, denied the fact during the interrogations he underwent in Constantinople; but it is no less true that in those he underwent in Bologna he admitted being in possession of the aforementioned counterfeit caimes.

In support of this admission, the authorities of Bologna are now requesting a specimen of these thirty-two counterfeit caimes and another of the six hundred counterfeit paper notes which the accomplices in this operation, Raphael Boschi and Ludovico Boschi, had previously introduced into the capital. However, as the thirty-two counterfeit caimes have since been burned, as is customary, at the Imperial Treasury along with other counterfeit items, it is not possible to comply with this request of the said authorities.

As for the six hundred counterfeit caimes previously sent by Facchini’s accomplices to their associates in Constantinople, these, having been the subject of dealings by the culprits imprisoned here, could not be seized by the Imperial Police.

Apart from the foregoing, it is moreover established and confirmed by the interrogations carried out in Bologna and by other documents communicated by Mr. Tkiades to the Imperial Ministry of Police that, with the exception of one of the accused, the seven others have confessed to being the authors of the manufacture of the counterfeit caimes, so that no reason would justify the release of the guilty parties before the punishment they deserve has been inflicted upon them.

I also believe it my duty to observe, before concluding, that the Sublime Porte is convinced that the said authorities will not dare to resort to releasing these accused persons, all the more so since, by a judgment rendered in October 1857, the same authorities, even before the conviction of eight other individuals implicated in Bologna in the manufacture of counterfeit money, had ruled that the counterfeiters in question could not be released.

I must add, however, that, despite the existence of facts which argue in favor of punishing the said accused, if circumstances should render your presence in Bologna indispensable for the satisfactory conclusion of this affair, I authorize you to proceed there. Please accept, etc.
