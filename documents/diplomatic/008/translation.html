Translation of a note from the Ministry of the Interior dated March 1, 1858, and communicated on March 3.

In continuation of the matter concerning the fabrication of counterfeit Turkish paper money, the undersigned considers it his duty to inform the Ministry of Foreign Affairs that, based on certain indications, the Public Security Office (Questura) proceeded in recent days to seize, in a garden belonging to the house formerly occupied at Madonna del Pilone by Venanzi and Varallo Pandolfini, a suitcase which was found tied between two pieces of wood, and in which approximately 3,850 counterfeit banknotes of the aforementioned currency were discovered, the greater part of which still lacked the two seals necessary for them to be put into circulation.

In order that the judicial authority might be better able to determine whether the discovered suitcase truly belonged to the accused, the said office took care to have the key seized, which was found hidden, along with many others, in one of the rooms of Varallo’s residence.

All of this, together with the corresponding official report (procès-verbal), has been transmitted to the public prosecutor (Avocat fiscal).

My Lord,

Since my last report addressed to Your Excellency, dated February 25, No. 225, I have had the honor of receiving Your Excellency’s telegraphic dispatch of the 27th of the same month, requesting precise details concerning Spadafora and Mayner-Veiller. I already had in my possession the descriptions of these two individuals and had sent a copy to Your Excellency in my report of February 25, No. 225. However, I had not yet been able to obtain a definitive answer regarding the date of their departure from Turin.

I therefore immediately hastened to address an urgent request to the Sardinian Government in order to obtain this information, which I needed to respond to Your Excellency’s inquiries, and on the 28th I promptly sent Your Excellency a telegraphic dispatch containing the details you desired.

Herewith I have the honor to enclose a translation of the latest information transmitted to me by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs in response to the above-mentioned request. Upon reviewing it, Your Excellency will see that I was not mistaken in advancing the opinion that the two culprits had succeeded in escaping from Turin after the arrest of Venanzi.

Information received from the Consul General in Genoa, to whom I had instructed to carry out investigations, has confirmed the accuracy of the data I had managed to obtain.

I have therefore hastened to write to the Imperial Embassies in Vienna and Paris, requesting that they notify the Austrian and French authorities of the two fugitives in order to ensure their arrest should they seek refuge in the territories of those two countries.

I have also written in the same sense to the chancellor of the Consulate General in Livorno, to the Consul General in Marseille, and to that of Malta.

The Sardinian police are also continuing their searches within the country, so that there is reason to hope that their arrest may be achieved.

I believe that the Imperial Government, for its part, should send their descriptions to Alexandria, Tunis, Tripoli, and other ports of the Empire, in order to have them apprehended in the event—quite probable—that they may have succeeded in embarking at Genoa for the East.

The judicial investigation is proceeding actively before the examining magistrate, and within a few days I hope to be able to obtain communication of the documents and to transmit copies to Your Excellency.

In the meantime, I hasten to inform you that we have succeeded in discovering a leather bag containing several thousand counterfeit caimes, which the counterfeiters had not had time to destroy and which they had hidden in a vineyard located near the house they occupied.

The caïmés are currently in the hands of the judicial authorities.

Our efforts are now directed toward discovering the stamps and seals that they used; they claim to have destroyed them, but nevertheless I am doing everything possible to ascertain whether this assertion is true and to succeed in getting hold of them if they still exist.

My report of February 24, No. 223, requested that Your Excellency kindly send me several specimens of genuine caimes as well as counterfeit caïmés seized from Calvocoressi, together with certificates attesting to their respective nature.

I renew my request to Your Excellency to kindly order that this shipment be sent to me without delay, as we will need to produce these items at trial.

I shall take care to keep Your Excellency informed of the progress of this affair, and I can assure you that I will neglect nothing here in order to achieve the punishment of the guilty.

I have the honor to be,
My Lord,
