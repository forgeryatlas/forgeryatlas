To His Excellency Fuad Pasha
Minister of Foreign Affairs in Constantinople

Turin, 8 December 1858
No. 363

Monseigneur,

For more than six months, and through several successive dispatches, I had requested the Imperial Ministry to kindly send me the documents that could serve as evidence for the prosecution against the individual named Bondesio, whom, by order of Your Excellency, I had caused to be arrested here on charges of fabricating counterfeit kaimes (paper currency).

To my great regret, I have received nothing, and I regret this all the more since the Sardinian Legation in Constantinople, to which the Sardinian Government had applied for the same purpose, has sent copies of two interrogations carried out in Constantinople of Bondesio’s accomplices.

I regret to have to inform Your Excellency today that the Attorney General, having found nothing in these two documents that could be invoked to establish Bondesio’s participation in the fabrication of counterfeit kaimes, and considering the impossibility in which this Embassy has found itself of producing documents to prove the guilt of this individual, and in view of the expiration of the legal period for preventive detention, was obliged to request from the Chamber of Indictments the release of Bondesio. This body, by an order dated 7 November, accepted the conclusions of the Public Prosecutor and ordered the release of this individual, which was carried out immediately.

The persistent lack of the documents and evidence that I had been requesting for so long has been the cause of this unfortunate outcome of our proceedings against Bondesio.

The individuals named Venanzi and Varallo Pandolfini are still in prison here under the charges we have brought against them. The judgment of their trial should take place soon, and with the evidence we currently possess, I hope that we will succeed in obtaining their conviction.

I have the honor to be,
Monseigneur,
of Your Excellency,
your very humble
and very obedient servant,

Rüstem
