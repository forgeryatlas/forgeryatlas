Turin, 5 April 1860

No. S. 736
No. S. 46

Monseigneur,

I have received the dispatch which Your Excellency was pleased to send me, dated the 21st of last March, No. 3915/30, transmitting the reply of the Imperial Ministry of Police to my report of 22 February last, No. 701, concerning the case of the counterfeiters of Bologna.

The annexation of the Romagna to Piedmont having now placed the authorities of Bologna under the direct authority of the Royal Ministry, I hastened to forward to Count Cavour all the details of this affair, to press him strongly against the release of the accused, to request their immediate return to the state prisons, and to urge the issuance of precise and strict orders so that the trial may be resumed and pursued with vigor.

I am awaiting information from Bologna, which I have requested concerning this matter, and should I deem it necessary, I will go there myself in order to advance the case and select a lawyer who can follow the proceedings.

Your Excellency may be assured that I will neglect nothing in order to bring about the punishment of the guilty, while keeping expenses as low as possible.

I have the honor to be,
Monseigneur,
Of Your Excellency,
the most humble
and most obedient servant,

Rüstem
