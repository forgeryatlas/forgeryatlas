Turin, 11 April 1861

No. G. 1009
No. S. 220

Your Highness,

I have just received and hasten to forward to Your Highness the translation of the order of indictment and of the bill of indictment concerning the individuals named Randaboschi, Crudeli, Facchini, and their accomplices, charged with the manufacture of counterfeit caimes, and who, by these judicial decisions, have been committed to the Court of Assizes of Bologna to be tried there.

The trial will probably take place toward the beginning of next May; I must therefore beg Your Highness to be so kind as to have sent to me, before that time, the final judgment delivered by the Supreme Court of Justice against the offenders arrested in Constantinople in the same case—a document which I had the honor to request by my report of 21 February last, No. 978/199, and the absence of which could still hinder the course of justice.

I shall, in concert with the lawyer and the attorney of the Imperial Embassy, attend to the necessary steps to secure the severe punishment of the guilty parties; and, should I deem it necessary, I would not hesitate to go myself to Bologna, not doubting that Your Highness would approve such a step if I considered it required in the interests of the Imperial Government.

Deign, Your Highness, to accept the assurances of my highest and most respectful consideration, with which I have the honor to be

Of Your Highness,
the very humble
and very obedient servant,

Rüstem
