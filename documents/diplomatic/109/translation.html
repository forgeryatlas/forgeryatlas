No. G. 846
No. T. 114

Turin, 16 August 1860

Your Excellency,

In my reports of 12 July (No. 823/96) and 26 of the same month (No. 837/107), I had the honor of informing Your Excellency of the repeated requests continually made by the judicial authorities of Bologna to finally be placed in possession of the originals and the translations of the records of the interrogations of the accused persons in Constantinople in the case of the counterfeiters of Bologna.

Your Excellency is already aware that, through energetic and persistent efforts, I have succeeded in having all the accused in Bologna—charged with having manufactured counterfeit caimes—returned to the state prisons, after they had been released by the previous government. The trial is to take place as soon as the Sublime Porte has returned, together with their translation, duly certified and legalized documents, the interrogations that I transmitted to it as early as 28 April.

The authorities of Bologna continue to demand these documents; they emphasize the cruelty of keeping people imprisoned indefinitely without trying them. On their side, the accused constantly voice bitter complaints, and the public—among whom they have friends and protectors—openly accuses us of hindering the progress of the trial by not producing the necessary documents.

Your Excellency will understand the difficult and awkward position in which I find myself placed. Dispatches from the Imperial Ministry of 23 May (No. 4145/45) and 6 June (No. 4225/48) announced the imminent sending of these documents; I therefore announced the same here, and I cannot now persuade anyone that it has taken four months to produce a translation.

I therefore find myself once again under the pressing necessity of urgently requesting that Your Excellency kindly have these papers sent to me without further delay; otherwise I fear that the matter may have an unfortunate outcome.

In any case, Your Excellency will do me the justice of recognizing that there is in no way any fault on my part.

Pray accept, Your Excellency, the assurances of my highest and most respectful consideration, with which I have the honor to remain,

Your Excellency’s
most humble and most devoted servant,

Rüstem
