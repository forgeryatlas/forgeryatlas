To His Excellency Fuad Pasha to Rüstem Bey, Chargé d’Affaires in Turin

March 30, 1859
No. 2315

(registered)

I have received and communicated to the Imperial Ministry of Police your report of the 10th of this month, No. 455, concerning the judgment rendered by the Criminal Court of Turin against the counterfeiters arrested in Sardinia, as well as the report of the Embassy official on the proceedings of this trial and the note from Mr. de Cavour attached thereto. Appreciating the importance of the result you have just obtained, despite all the difficulties, in this matter which concerns our finances, and the beneficial effect that this sentence will produce in the future,
I hasten to convey to you the full approval of the Imperial Government, together with the particular expression of my satisfaction. The arguments put forward by the defense of the accused and the reasons invoked by them in their pleadings do not seem to me to be of a nature to be accepted by the Court of Cassation or to lead to a new trial; however, in any case, I am convinced that the justice of our cause and the zeal you have shown in its defense will overcome any new obstacles and maintain the result obtained.

We therefore give our full approval to the donation you have made to the hospice of the poor in Turin of the sum that will be awarded as damages to the civil party, and we thank you for having, by this means, secured recognition of the principle of compensation and at the same time gained for the Imperial Government the sympathy of the public through this act of generosity.

Please accept, etc. etc.
