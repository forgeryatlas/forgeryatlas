His Excellency Safvet Effendi, Acting Minister of Foreign Affairs, Constantinople
Turin, 20 September 1860
N.G. 872
N.S. 126

Acknowledgment of receipt of the ministerial dispatch No. 4625/76 transmitting the translation and copy (or one of the interrogations) relating to the trial of the counterfeiters of Bologna.

Excellency,

I have received the dispatch which Your Excellency did me the honor of sending me, dated the 5th of this month, No. 4625/76, transmitting to me the translations as well as the original of the interrogations of the individuals arrested in the capital, charged with having fabricated or put into circulation false caimes. In forwarding these documents to the judicial authorities of Bologna, I am neglecting nothing in order to arrive as soon as possible at the resolution of this trial.

Your Excellency will have noted that, in the note of which I had the honor to transmit a copy through my report of 23 August No. 851/117, Count Cavour requests to receive an authenticated copy and a faithful translation of the judgments which may have been pronounced in this matter by the Ottoman courts. This request is made on the basis of a formal petition by the defense lawyers of the individuals arrested in Bologna. Now, as it is to be expected that these lawyers will take advantage of the absence of such documents to once again hinder the progress of the trial, I urgently beg Your Excellency to be so kind as to place me in a position to overcome this new difficulty by sending me, as soon as possible, authenticated copies as well as duly legalized translations of all the judgments pronounced by the courts of Constantinople in the matter in question.

Kindly accept, Excellency, the assurances of my highest and most respectful consideration, with which I have the honor to remain,

Your Excellency’s
most humble and most obedient servant,

Rüstem
