To His Excellency Fuad Pasha to Musurus Bey, Ambassador in London

November 17, 1858
No. 1905

Sir Ambassador,

The Imperial Ministry has received your dispatch of October 24 last, containing a copy of the report that was sent to you by our Consul in New York, dated the 7th of the same month, concerning the counterfeit caimes (banknotes) manufactured by a certain Mr. Harison in New York; as well as the two caimes and the justificatory article published in American newspapers by the latter.

Taking into consideration the circumstances under which this fabrication was carried out—circumstances which considerably lessen the culpability of Mr. Harison—we request you, Sir Ambassador, to transmit to our Consul in New York the order to first destroy all the counterfeit caimes that have been seized by having them burned in the presence of the local authorities; and then, without delay, to send to Constantinople the machines and tools that were found, consisting of 22 tinted printing plates, 16 plain printing plates, 27 dies imitating the tughras, and 16 treasury seals—81 items in total—which were used in this production. At the same time, instruct him not to pursue further the legal action initiated against Mr. Harison, who appears to have been unaware of the seriousness of the act in which he became complicit.

Please accept, etc.
