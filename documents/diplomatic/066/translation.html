No. 173

Naples, April 18, 1859

Excellency,

I have the honor to acknowledge receipt by Your Excellency of two notes which you were pleased to address to me, dated March 23 (Nos. 2320 and 2321), and of a third dated the 30th, bearing No. 2350. In accordance with note No. 2320, I have received the various samples
of caimes and I am in the process of making the fullest possible use of them for the discovery of the counterfeiters who are believed to be established in Naples.

I have the honor to inform Your Excellency that Mr. Tkiades left Bologna on the 6th of this month to go to Venice; before his departure he was able to attend the trial of the accused, and he is taking with him the case file and the documentary evidence.

As I have already had the honor of informing Your Excellency, I had arranged for the delivery of a sum of three thousand francs (200 Roman scudi — ?), (which Mr. Rothschild had advanced during his stay in Naples; but, not having received by telegraphic dispatch the instructions given in this regard to Your Excellency, I took it upon myself to forward the sum of 3,000 francs, from which I had deducted the amount of two hundred francs that he had paid at the time of his departure from Naples.)

In the event that Your Excellency should be pleased to approve my conduct in this matter, I have the honor to be, Your Excellency’s most humble and most obedient servant,

Spitzer
