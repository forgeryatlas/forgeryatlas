Turin, 23 August 1860

Excellency,

My report of the 16th of this month, No. 846/114, reiterated to Your Excellency my most urgent requests for the immediate forwarding of the records of the interrogations of the individuals arrested in Constantinople in the case of the counterfeiters of Bologna.

Today it is my duty to transmit to Your Excellency a copy of a new note which I have just received on this subject from the Sardinian Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

Upon reading this document, Your Excellency will be able to appreciate the insistence with which the Royal Ministry continues to demand the dispatch of these documents, which are indispensable for the resolution of the trial in question.

Your Excellency will also see, with equal interest, the request for authenticated copies and translations of the judgments that may have been rendered in this matter by the Ottoman courts.

I must therefore once again beg Your Excellency to be so good as to issue peremptory orders for the forwarding of these documents without further delay.

Kindly accept, Excellency, the assurances of my highest and most respectful consideration, with which I have the honor to remain,

Your Excellency’s most humble and most obedient servant,

Rüstem
