Turin 14 April 1859

N° 497

Monseigneur,

I hasten to inform Your Excellency that, by a judgment of the 9th of this month, the Supreme Court of Cassation has rejected the appeal filed by Venanzi.

Consequently, the sentence pronounced by the Court of Appeal of Turin against Venanzi and Varallo Pandolfini—by which these counterfeiters were condemned to eight years’ imprisonment—is now definitively confirmed and will be fully and entirely carried out. Thus concluded the procedure which, by order of the Imperial Government, I had initiated here against the above-mentioned individuals and their accomplices.

Although the judgment of the Court of Appeal has, in principle, admitted the claim for compensation put forward by our lawyer and has referred the case to the civil courts to determine the amount, I believe it would not be appropriate to initiate a new civil action for this purpose, especially considering that, according to all the information I have obtained, Venanzi and Varallo are completely without means and that we would never be able to obtain payment of the sum that might be awarded to us; such proceedings would therefore only entail useless legal expenses.

I hope that Your Excellency will approve my view, and I await your decision. Soon I shall forward to Your Excellency a statement of the sums we must pay for legal costs, including court fees and the fees of solicitors and attorneys, etc. etc.

I have the honor to be, Monseigneur,
Your Excellency’s most humble and most obedient servant,

Rüstem
