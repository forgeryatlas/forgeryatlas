To His Excellency Fuad Pasha, Minister of Foreign Affairs, Constantinople

Monseigneur,

I have had the honor of receiving the dispatch which Your Excellency was pleased to address to me, dated the 9th of this month, No. 4082/44, transmitting the certificate requested by the authorities of Bologna establishing the precise date on which the new penal code began to be in force in the Ottoman Empire.

I hastened at once to forward this document to the judicial authorities of Bologna through the intermediary of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

I am pleased by the approval which Your Excellency has been kind enough to express regarding the steps I have taken to bring about the trial of the counterfeiters in question.

From my previous reports, Your Excellency will have seen that I did not consider it necessary to prolong my stay in Bologna; I had achieved the objective I had set myself, namely to ascertain how it came about that the case had been stalled for so long, and to remove the obstacles preventing it from reaching a definitive outcome. Having attained this goal, it was unnecessary to prolong my stay, especially as we had to await from Constantinople the requested documents, and since only after their arrival could the investigation be definitively closed, the report of the public prosecutor drawn up, and the case placed on the court docket.

It is customary to grant a certain period to the lawyers of the accused to prepare their defense, and this period could scarcely be less than two months from the day on which they are given access to the indictment and the case file. I therefore beg Your Excellency to hasten as much as possible the sending of the translation of the interrogations of the accused in Constantinople, a document which is still awaited in order to be able to close the investigation.

As soon as I receive it, I shall vigorously press forward the progress of the trial. In the meantime, I continue my efforts with the Royal Ministry to obtain the re-incarceration, under lock and key, of the accused who, during the time of the Provisional Government of the Romagna, were provisionally released on bail. I have most recently addressed, for this purpose, a new and very forceful note to Count Cavour, and I hope that it will produce the effect I seek; failing that, I will probably be obliged to address to him a formal protest and to report the matter to Your Excellency.

I have the honor to be, Monseigneur, Your Excellency’s most humble and most obedient servant,

Rüstem
