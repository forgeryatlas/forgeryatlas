Turin, February 24, 1858.

No. 223

My Lord,

At the time of the arrest of the counterfeiters Venanzi and his accomplices, the culprits, having been warned—either through the imprudence or the complicity of a police agent—that they were being sought by the police, had had time to burn all their papers and the counterfeit caimes already produced (which must have been in great number, judging by the large quantity of ashes that was found), and to destroy or make disappear the printing stones, stamps, and seals.

Providence, which does not allow crime to go unpunished, had caused Venanzi to forget, in the pocket of his overcoat, a specimen of a counterfeit caime, which was thus able to be seized on him.

This specimen is one of the principal pieces of evidence that must be produced at trial; now, in order to prove to the judges that it is false, we require comparative material.

I therefore request Your Excellency kindly to have sent to me without delay:

1° several specimens of genuine twenty-piastre caimes of the same issue as those falsified by the counterfeiters. These caïmés must be attached by a ribbon or cord to a sheet of paper, and the ends of the cord sealed under an official seal. The sheet of paper on which they are thus attached must bear a certificate from the Sublime Porte declaring that the attached caïmés are genuine banknotes issued by the Imperial Government and circulating as legal currency.

2° several specimens of the counterfeit caimes seized from Calvocoressi, arranged in the same manner on a sheet of paper bearing a certificate stating that these are counterfeit caïmés fraudulently made in imitation of genuine notes of the Imperial Treasury and seized from Calvocoressi at the time of his arrest in Constantinople.

I consider it unnecessary to point out to Your Excellency that these certificates must be carefully prepared and sent to me as soon as possible.

I have the honor to be,
My Lord,
Your Excellency’s
most humble and most obedient servant,

Rüstem
