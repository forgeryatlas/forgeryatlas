His Excellency Fuad Pasha to Spitzer Effendi in Naples
July 20, 1859
No. 2789

Mr. Chargé d’Affaires,

Having forwarded to the Ministry of Police your report of May 9 last (No. 182), indicating the urgency of sending the documents which the Director of Police in Bologna is awaiting in order to complete the proceedings initiated against the authors of counterfeit caimés, this Ministry has now communicated to me, in reply, the report submitted to it on this matter by the Criminal Court of Investigation.

This Court has learned from Mr. Tkiades that the documents requested by the Director of Police of Bologna are:

1° extracts from the interrogations undergone by the individuals arrested here as actors or counterfeiters and detained at Bologna, extracts which will serve as information for the magistrates of Bologna; and

2° the translation of the articles concerning counterfeiters from the penal code as it stood two years ago and as it is in force today in Turkey.

In response to the request for extracts or information, the Criminal Court of Investigation explains that the accused detained here number four, namely Ludovico Boschi, Cesare Venzi, Roberto Diamante, an Austrian protégé, and Andonaki Dragonicos, a merchant and a British protégé.

The first of these, through contradictory statements, had at the outset alternately confessed and denied his participation in the crime; later aligning himself with the system of absolute and persistent denials adopted by his three companions, it became necessary to produce before them the written and signed confessions of the detainees in Bologna.

However, upon seeing these confessions, they replied that although those individuals had named them, they would, if confronted together, merely declare that they were not their associates, since they had never had any connection with them.

Given the difficulties that would arise in arranging a personal confrontation between the accused in Bologna and those here, the Criminal Court of Investigation had photographic portraits taken of the latter in order to send them to Bologna. When it came to the said Ludovico Boschi, however, he uttered threats against the painter (photographer), which constitutes a circumstance particularly indicative of his guilt; his portrait was therefore considered henceforth superfluous and abandoned.

The Director of Police of Bologna, upon receiving these photographs, is requested to present them to the detainees, to question them as to the name of each person, the role each played in the association, and the places where they had been together, and to have sent back here the photographic portraits, each accompanied by a written statement signed by the said detainees and duly authenticated.

Independently of this matter, the Criminal Court of Investigation, in the same report, expresses the desire that the Director of Police of Bologna be informed that, having consulted the archives concerning one Ulysse Baldini (Antonio), arrested in Bologna on the representations of Mr. Tkiades, it appears that the confessions made by this accused—namely that, being part of a company of counterfeiters formed at Constantinople two years earlier by one G. B. Bongiacci (Giovanni Battista Bongiaci) and other known individuals, he himself had engraved the tughra and the seals of the latter, by means of which he had fabricated and put into circulation counterfeit caimes—are fully confirmed by the interrogations previously conducted in Constantinople of the said G. B. Bongiacci Giovanni Battista Bongiaci and his associates, who were sentenced under the former penal code to three years of forced labor.

Such, in substance, Mr. Chargé d’Affaires, is the report of the Criminal Court of Investigation which the Minister of Police has just transmitted to me; and in communicating it to you in turn, I am sending you herewith the three aforementioned photographs as well as the translation of the articles concerning counterfeiters, drawn both from the former penal code and from that which has been recently promulgated and is now in force.

In the hope that you are now in a position to satisfy the wishes of the magistrates of Bologna with regard to this affair of the counterfeiters, I avail myself, etc. etc.
