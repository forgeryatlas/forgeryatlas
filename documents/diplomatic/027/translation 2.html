To His Excellency
Mahmoud Pasha
Acting Minister of Foreign Affairs

No. 325
Paris, 13 August 1858

Monseigneur,

It is my duty to once again call Your Excellency’s attention to the case of the lawyer Bondesio, arrested in Turin at the formal request of the Imperial Government on charges of fraudulent accounting.

Through considerable efforts, we have so far succeeded in keeping this individual in prison, although the documents that could serve to prove his guilt and secure his conviction have not yet reached us. However, Bondesio’s friends are making persistent efforts to have him released; they have succeeded in mobilizing the press, which has taken up the case and accuses the examining magistrate of violating the laws of the country by detaining a citizen in prison for four or five months without sufficient evidence and without referring him to the competent courts.

The position of this magistrate is therefore becoming extremely difficult, and he does not cease to press us to produce our evidence, warning us that if we delay any longer, he will be forced to pronounce a dismissal of the case (a decision of no grounds for prosecution). Now, the release of Bondesio would be highly regrettable: first, because we would thus allow a forger to escape the punishment he deserves; secondly, because of the harmful effect this would produce, since, believing themselves henceforth assured of impunity, scoundrels of Bondesio’s kind would be encouraged to imitate his example; and finally, because Bondesio would probably bring a lawsuit against us to claim damages.

I therefore once again beg Your Excellency to be so kind as to issue the most peremptory orders so that, without further delay, copies and certified translations of the interrogations of all of Bondesio’s accomplices—who have identified him as one of the forgers—as well as all other documents that may serve as evidence against him, be sent to me from Constantinople.

I dare to hope that Your Excellency will recognize the great importance of producing these documents in legal and official form as soon as possible, and will kindly give the necessary orders accordingly.

I have the honor to be, Monseigneur,
of Your Excellency,
your very humble and very obedient servant,

Rüstem
