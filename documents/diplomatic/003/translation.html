His Excellency Fuad Pasha
Minister of Foreign Affairs Constantinople

Turin, February 18, 1858.

My Lord,

Yesterday, via Trieste, I had the honor of sending to Your Excellency my report concerning the arrest of the counterfeiters whom Your Excellency had indicated to me in your encrypted telegraphic dispatch of the 10th of this month.

For the sake of regularity, I am sending herewith, via Genoa, a duplicate of my dispatch and of the annex that accompanied it.

I hastened to address an official communication to the President of the Council, Minister of Foreign Affairs, in order to thank him for the communication he had just made to me, praise the diligence shown by the police on this occasion, and request that he kindly give the necessary orders so that the Imperial Embassy may be regularly informed of the progress of the proceedings that are to be initiated against the accused, in order to follow them through their various stages and keep the Imperial Government accurately informed.

My aim has thus been to be able to monitor the actions of the authorities and the courts, and to avoid, as occurred in the Mestrallet affair, that a glaring injustice might be done to us (see my report to His Highness Ali Pasha dated December 3, No. 217, to which I have not yet received a reply).

Tomorrow I am to meet one of the principal lawyers of Turin in order to consult him on the steps that Sardinian law allows us to take to ensure the conviction of the guilty parties, and to determine whether we have the right to constitute ourselves as a civil party and to appoint a lawyer to prosecute in the name of the Imperial Government.

I shall have the honor of informing Your Excellency of the result of this consultation and of requesting your instructions.

I have the honor to be,
My Lord,
Your Excellency’s
most humble and obedient servant,

Rüstem
