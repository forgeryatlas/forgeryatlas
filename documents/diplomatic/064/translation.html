Turin, April 7, 1859

No. 495

Monseigneur,

As I had the honor of informing Your Excellency in my report of March 17, No. 469, Mr. Tkiades had informed me by telegraph of three individuals implicated in the affair of the counterfeit caimes of Bologna, and I immediately hastened to take the necessary steps to obtain from the Sardinian Government that searches be carried out in order to secure their arrest in the event that they might be found within that Kingdom.

I hasten to transmit herewith to Your Excellency a copy of a communication which I have recently received from the Sardinian Ministry of Foreign Affairs, informing me that two of these individuals had been arrested at Corfu and subsequently released because they had departed for Malta.

I immediately sent a copy of this communication to Mr. Tkiades, to the Ottoman Consul General in Malta, as well as to the Consul General in Genoa, to whom I gave instructions to keep watch on arrivals so as to be able to bring these malefactors to justice if they persist in their original intention of coming to Piedmont. I have the honor to be, Monseigneur, Your Excellency’s most humble and most obedient servant, Rüstem
