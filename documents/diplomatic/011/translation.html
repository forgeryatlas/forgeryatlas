His Excellency
Fuad Pasha, Minister of Foreign Affairs
Constantinople

Turin, March 11, 1858

No. 257

My Lord,

The investigation of the case concerning the counterfeiters Venanzi and Varallo-Pandolfini is proceeding in a regular manner.

The examining magistrate has completed his investigations and, for the past two days, has submitted to the Royal prosecutor his report and all the documents of the case.

I immediately addressed a note to the Ministry of Foreign Affairs requesting that the report be communicated to me in time so that I could transmit it by this courier to the Sublime Porte. However, the departure time of the mail is approaching and I have not yet received anything.

We must from now on concern ourselves with assembling the evidence that can ensure the conviction of the guilty parties. In addition to the specimens of genuine and counterfeit caimes, accompanied by authentic certificates, which I have already had the honor of requesting Your Excellency to send me, I must also request that you kindly give the most formal orders that there be sent to me, without delay, authenticated and legalized copies of the interrogations of Calvocoressi and Brescianini, and that all the other documents of the investigation and trial of these two criminals be sent to me progressively. One may even send me all these documents in Turkish in order to avoid the loss of time necessary to translate them in Constantinople; we are perfectly capable of doing so here.

Your Excellency will understand the important role that these documents must play here in the trial of Venanzi and Varallo-Pandolfini; their value may add new evidence to those we already possess and strengthen conviction in the minds of the judges and jurors.

I have the honor to be,
My Lord,
Your Excellency’s
most humble
and most obedient servant,

Rüstem
