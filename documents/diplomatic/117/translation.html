Turin, 27 December 1860

To His Highness Aali Pasha, Acting Minister of Foreign Affairs in Constantinople

No. G. 951
No. S. 180

Your Highness,

As I had the honor to inform Your Highness in my report of the 20th of this month, No. 949/179, the trial of the persons accused of manufacturing counterfeit caimes in Bologna did not take place on the 17th as had been مقرر (fixed). The illness of one of the judges, together with the new situation created for me by the persistence of the judicial authorities in considering me as a civil party—a position that obliges me to take an active part in the proceedings—have been the reasons for postponing the case to another hearing.

Enclosed, I have the honor to submit to Your Highness a copy of the summary of the investigation of the case, drawn up by the Crown Prosecutor for the information of the Court. Your Highness will see therein that the Public Prosecutor does not even cast doubt on the guilt of the accused.

I am presently awaiting an official communication from the Ministry of Justice, by virtue of which I shall have to take the necessary steps in my capacity as a civil party; however, as on the first of January next the Sardinian Penal Code is to come into force in the Romagna, I believe that the entire investigation of the case will have to be recommenced in accordance with the Sardinian Code of Criminal Procedure. The trial will therefore probably be delayed once again, which will give me time to receive a copy of the judgment delivered in the same matter by the tribunals of Constantinople, the transmission of which I have requested in my previous reports.

Please accept, Your Highness, the assurances of my highest and most respectful consideration, with which I have the honor to be,

Of Your Highness,
your very humble
and very obedient servant,

Rüstem
