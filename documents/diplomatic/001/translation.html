Turin, February 15, 1858.

Sir Chargé d’Affaires,

I hasten to inform you of the initial and satisfactory results of the investigations ordered without delay by the Royal Department of the Interior, following your letter of the 11th of this month, concerning the search for and arrest of three individuals suspected of engaging, in this capital, in the manufacture of counterfeit Turkish paper money.

It was first established that a certain so-called Mircovich, a printer in Sassari, apparently about 35 years old, had recently ordered from a paper factory a certain quantity of paper of a particular quality and shade; that he had the sample remade three times before finding it to his liking, explaining that he insisted greatly on the exact shade (inclining toward yellow), because the paper was to be used for the continuation of a work begun on similar paper; that once the order was completed, the paper was delivered to the said Mircovich at his residence, Rue du Bourg Neuf No. 21, but that this individual had since disappeared.

The police proceeded without delay to the above-mentioned address and established that it was the residence of a man named Venanzi Alexandre, and of another individual whose description matched that of the so-called Mircovich, but both had been absent for some time. It was nevertheless learned that they had likely withdrawn to some rural locality in the vicinity of this capital, and measures having been taken accordingly, it was possible to discover and arrest the said Venanzi Alexandre yesterday.

From him it was learned that he lived in a house located at Madonna del Pilone, near Turin, together with a certain Pandolfini, a former soldier. A thorough search was immediately carried out at their residence and led to the discovery of a sample of counterfeit Turkish paper money, various instruments used for printing it, such as a press, etc., and a large quantity of the paper mentioned above.

In addition to Venanzi, the police also arrested Mr. Luigi Varallo Pandolfini, former major, native of Moncalvo (Province of Casale), aged 41, and his wife, Mrs. Antoinette Bernard, aged 24.

There is thus reason to believe that the principal members of this criminal association have been apprehended and that their criminal plans have been analyzed; further police investigations, which are being pursued with care and activity, will undoubtedly complete the results obtained.

Meanwhile, I have no doubt that you will be pleased to learn of these discoveries and arrests, and I ask you to accept, Sir Chargé d’Affaires, etc.

The Secretary General (Signed) De Salmour

to Rüstem Bey Chargé d’Affaires of Turkey
