His Excellency Fuad Pasha, Minister of Foreign Affairs in Constantinople
Turin, January 26, 1860
No. 689

Monseigneur,

I have had the honor of receiving the dispatch which Your Excellency was pleased to write to me on the 4th of this month (No. 3545), informing me that you had forwarded to the Imperial Ministry of Police my report of December 15 last (No. 647), concerning the counterfeiters arrested at Bologna, urging it for the second time to transmit without delay the documents necessary to establish the guilt of the accused and bring about their conviction.

According to certain information that has reached me, it appears to me that the authorities of Bologna show little disposition to render us justice, and even display some inclination to take advantage of the delay in the arrival of the documents they had requested, in order to attempt to set the accused at liberty.

I therefore beg Your Excellency to be so good as to press once more upon the Ministry of Police for the immediate dispatch of these documents. I have written to Bologna, and within a few days I hope to receive precise information that will enable me to judge the disposition of the authorities of that city. It seems to me that my presence in Bologna could be useful in bringing about a prompt resolution of this matter; I would therefore need Your Excellency to be kind enough to send me authorization to go there, should the information I am expecting lead me to consider it necessary to make a short visit—one which, moreover, might have the advantage of allowing me to judge for myself the real state of affairs in Central Italy and to render an accurate account thereof to Your Excellency.

I have the honor to be, Monseigneur,
Your Excellency’s most humble and most obedient servant,

Rüstem

Turin le 26 janvier 1860.

N° 688.

Monseigneur,

Ainsi que Votre Excellence a bien voulu me l’annoncer par sa dépêche du 28 décembre dernier, N° 3488, Monsieur Alleon m’ayant fait parvenir la somme de mille quatre cent cinq francs soixante-cinq centimes, je me suis empressé de solder les comptes de Monsieur l’Avocat Lecchio, qui a plaidé pour nous dans l’affaire des faux monnayeurs Venanzi et Varallo, et de Monsieur l’Avoué Varya, notre fondé de pouvoirs dans cette affaire.

Ci-annexe, je me fais un devoir, conformément au désir que Votre Excellence m’a exprimé dans sa dépêche susmentionnée, de lui remettre les deux comptes dûment acquittés et légalisés.

J’ai l’honneur d’être, Monseigneur,

De Votre Excellence, Le très humble, et très obéissant serviteur

Rüstem

Translation

Turin, January 26, 1860
No. 688

Monseigneur,

As Your Excellency was pleased to inform me in your dispatch of December 28 last (No. 3488), Mr. Alleon having forwarded to me the sum of one thousand four hundred and five francs and sixty-five centimes, I hastened to settle the accounts of Mr. Lecchio, the lawyer who pleaded on our behalf in the case of the counterfeiters Venanzi and Varallo, and of Mr. Varya, the solicitor who acted as our representative in this matter.

I enclose herewith, in accordance with the wish Your Excellency expressed in the aforementioned dispatch, the two accounts, duly settled and legalized.

I have the honor to be, Monseigneur,
Your Excellency’s most humble and most obedient servant,

Rüstem
